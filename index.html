Project 1 · Lab 4 + Features

Nature Hub

Problem Statement Plant biodiversity information is often scattered and difficult for students to access in an organised way. This reduces awareness and understanding of plant diversity in maintaining ecosystems. Nature Hub provides a simple, structured platform to explore plant families, genera, and species interactively.
Lessons Learned Successfully built a multi-screen application using Jetpack Compose and Navigation. I learned how to share and hoist state using a centralized ViewModel to ensure user data survives screen rotations.

Learning Journey

Reflection

From Lab 1 to Project 2, my journey evolved from building simple static UIs to developing a complete, data-driven Android ecosystem. I overcame significant technical challenges, particularly in managing complex state across more than 7 screens and implementing advanced hardware sensors via Device Camera.

Building Nature Hub demonstrated how mobile technology can directly impact SDG 15 by promoting biodiversity awareness, plant education, and digital conservation practices. Learning to integrate Room for local persistence, Firebase for cloud syncing, and Retrofit for live APIs has given me the confidence and skills to architect production-ready software.

Technology becomes meaningful when it connects people back to the natural world — Nature Hub is my contribution to that bridge.
